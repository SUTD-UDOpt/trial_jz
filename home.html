Task

This work-in-progress tool supports urban planners to generate likely development scenarios for land parcels in Singapore. Choose a parcel and use this optimization tool to find the most satisfactory building solution. The optimization objectives tested here are building orientation and view obstruction. The Gross Plot Ratio (GPR) of each parcel is fixed. You will test 2 different optimization interfaces: an advanced interface and a simple interface. You will be able to rerun each optimization multiple times.

You can read this task again by clicking the help button at the top right corner of the screen.

Instruction for simple optimization
  • Select a parcel that you wish to optimize by clicking on the available parcels. Click the “FINALIZE” button when you are done with your selection.
  • Please input the value of each parameter. In this case, determine how narrow or broad you would like the search for solutions to be.
  • Broader search will likely return more results but will take longer to optimize.
  • To begin the optimization process, click the “OPTIMIZE” button.
  • You can read these instructions again by clicking the help button at the top right corner of the screen.
Instruction for simple optimization results
  • You will find a list of result buttons after the optimization is completed.
  • You can toggle between these buttons to visualize the generated solutions on the parcel.
  • If you find a solution that you find satisfactory, you can click the “SUBMIT” button to key in your chosen solution.
  • If you do not find any satisfactory result, you can click the “RESET” button to redefine your parameters and rerun the optimization.
  • You can read these instructions again by clicking the help button at the top right corner of the screen.
Instruction for advanced optimization
  • Select a parcel that you wish to optimize by clicking on the available parcels. Click the “FINALIZE” button when you are done with your selection.
  • In this section, the setting is divided into 3 groups: “Model Parameters”, “Objective Weights” and “Optimization Settings”
  • Within “Model Parameters,” you will find 5 sliders. BKeyXScale and BKeyYScale sliders define the length and width of the selected block keys for optimization respectively. You can see which block keys are being selected on the interface.
  • GridAngle and GridSpacing sliders define the rotation and size of the grid that will divide the parcel and hence define where the block keys can be built within the parcel
  • ParcelStoreyScale slider defines the range of building heights that will be searched. Each parcel has its own GPR target, so the shorter the buildings are, the greater number of buildings will be built within the parcel.
  • Number of generation and population defines the generation and population of the optimization. For a decent optimization time, it is advised to keep the numbers smaller or equal to 10.
  • Objective Weights and optimization settings are optional. Weight inputs will turn the optimization process into a weighted single objective optimization. You will be asked to input a weight between 1 and 100 for each optimization objective. The weight of an objective defines its importance relative to the other objectives.
  • Crossover is a genetic algorithm GA operator controlling the extent to which the parameters of two parent variants are mixed to generate new offspring. It generates stochastically new solutions from an existing population. 100% crossover will result in all members of the next generation created via crossover. 0% cross over will result in all members of the next generation copied from the previous generation, with no mixing.
  • Mutation is a genetic algorithm GA operator that applies changes randomly to variant parameters to produce new variants. Mutation rate controls the number of parameters that are randomly changed.
  • To begin the optimization process, click the “OPTIMIZE” button.
  • You can read these instructions again by clicking the help button at the top right corner of the screen.
Instruction for advanced optimization results
  • You will find a list of result buttons after the optimization is completed.
  • You can toggle between these buttons to visualize the generated solutions on the parcel.
  • Objective 1 (NS Orientation): Minimize façade exposure to east-west orientation for reducing heating due to direct sun exposure. A score of 0.5 means building footprint has twice as much of building façade facing NS than EW.
  • Objective 2 (View Obstruction): Minimize view obstruction from units by penalizing any building placed too close to another building. A score of 100 means a sum of 100 obstructions found within 90m from all units.
  • If you find a solution that you find satisfactory, you can click the “SUBMIT” button to key in your chosen solution.
  • If you do not find any satisfactory result, you can click the “RESET” button to redefine your parameters and rerun the optimization.
  • You can read these instructions again by clicking the help button at the top right corner of the screen.
